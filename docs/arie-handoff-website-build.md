## What This Project Is

LuxVeritas.media is the institutional front door for Lux Veritas across:

- music
- film and television
- events
- Codex
- submissions
- press and contact
- private portal access
- future commerce
- future membership and community

The public site is meant to:

- sell the world
- establish trust and taste
- capture interest and access requests
- convert fans into members, attendees, contributors, and aligned partners

The public site is not meant to:

- expose backend or operational machinery
- publish private investor or partner materials
- reveal unreleased internal systems
- expose internal campaign logic, release operations, rights administration, or governance frameworks

## Source Material History

There were two major rounds of strategy/design inputs:

1. Initial design and strategy pack
2. A second pack that introduced deeper operating-system, rights, campaign, and portal architecture

That second round expanded the vision into:

- release operations
- rights and royalties governance
- campaign management
- command-center thinking
- Codex gating
- portal architecture
- future commerce and member infrastructure

However, a major product decision was made:

> The public site must not behave like an exposed operating manual.

So we used the strategic depth to shape the internal architecture, but we intentionally removed most operational language from the public-facing layer.

## High-Level Evolution Of The Site

### Phase 1: Single-Page Institutional Prototype

Early build direction was a more brochure-like institutional homepage with:

- hero
- music / film / events / Codex positioning
- access and submissions forms

### Phase 2: Multi-Page Expansion

The site was expanded into a generated static multi-route build. This added:

- distinct public pages
- event microsites
- public Codex plus gated Codex shells
- portal shells
- utility pages
- commerce / works / community placeholders

### Phase 3: Access-Control Cleanup

The biggest structural change was repositioning the site away from public ops exposure and toward a cleaner access model:

- **Public** = story, trust, releases, atmosphere, access request, fan conversion
- **Gated** = membership, creator, portal, investor, private drops, private tools
- **Internal** = operations, finance, rights, prompts, canon governance, internal strategy, dashboards, unreleased control surfaces

This became the main rule set for all later edits.

## Key Build Decisions

### 1. Public Nav Was Tightened

Current top nav:

- Home
- Music
- Film
- Events
- Codex
- About
- Join

This exceeds the original six-item Phase 1 note by one item, but `Join` was intentionally promoted because conversion needed a visible public access point.

### 2. Footer Became Utility Navigation

Current footer:

- Works
- Store
- Membership
- Submissions
- Press
- Contact
- Privacy
- Terms

Items like Portal, Investor, Creator, Licensing, Ledger, and hidden internal areas were removed from the public nav.

### 3. Public Site Must Sell The World, Not The Machine

This became the central copy rule.

Public pages were rewritten to emphasize:

- listen
- watch
- join
- attend
- collect
- create

Instead of exposing:

- pipelines
- tooling
- dashboards
- campaign mechanics
- backend implementation

### 4. Gated Routes Were Preserved As Shells

Instead of deleting future or sensitive areas entirely, many were turned into:

- request-access shells
- private-access shells
- `noindex, nofollow` routes

This preserved architecture without exposing the underlying system.

### 5. Legal Pages Were Moved From Placeholder To Draft

Privacy and Terms no longer use placeholder/template language. They now contain draft-safe legal structure, but still require review before production launch.

## Copy And UX Iteration Summary

Below is the most important iteration history by area.

### Home

Original direction leaned more institutional and system-oriented.

Current direction:

- hero stays institutional
- operating-system framing was replaced with a fan-journey / world-entry section
- current movement:
  - Listen to the signal.
  - Watch the world open.
  - Join the circle.
  - Attend the room.
  - Collect the drop.
  - Create from the source.
- CTA language shifted to:
  - `Enter Lux Veritas`
  - `Join for first access`

### Music

Original direction exposed more release-operations flavor.

Current direction:

- music is the first doorway into the world
- built around listen / watch / join
- release mechanics were removed
- copy now emphasizes intentional releases, protection, and longevity

### SPMVP

Original route had more campaign-hub / release-room framing.

Current direction:

- public release doorway only
- listen / watch / join
- early access framing
- no ad-safe wrapper, tracking logic, or campaign mechanics language

### Film

Original direction risked revealing more development-slate style framing.

Current direction:

- cinematic worlds and mythologies
- public chapters only
- deeper lore opens by access
- unreleased story systems are not public

### Events

Current direction:

- rooms with intention
- public pages show atmosphere, not ops
- exact details open only after access

### Codex

One of the clearest structural decisions:

- Outer Codex = public
- Inner Codex = members / approved creators
- Sanctum = internal only

Public Codex page must tease access structure without revealing deeper materials.

### Portal / Sign In

These were rewritten into minimal private-access shells.

Important decision:

- do not expose backend-stack language
- do not explain internal auth architecture publicly

### Submissions

Current public behavior:

- screened intake
- clear limitations
- no confidential material unless invited
- no obligation or guarantee language included

### Contact

Current direction:

- aligned inquiries only
- route message after review

### Ledger

Major decision:

- split conceptually into public ledger-lite and internal ledger
- public page is now trust / credit / protection philosophy only
- operational specifics removed

### Membership

Original placeholder became a real conversion page.

Current direction:

- waitlist / conversion
- early access
- private drops
- listening rooms
- presales
- behind-the-scenes releases
- deeper Codex / lore
- exclusive merch

### Community

Current direction:

- public join layer
- real feature set remains gated
- owned fan identity framing

### Investor

Major constraint:

- request-access only
- no deck
- no numbers
- no projections
- no use-of-funds
- no public data-room language

### Legal

Original placeholders were unacceptable for launch.

Current direction:

- draft-safe structure
- no placeholder text
- still marked internally for legal review
